Project ideas

A lot of the 'ideas' I have for things I'd like to do aren't creating my own projects, but usually while I'm doing something I'll think of a way to improve whatever system I'm using at the given time.

The biggest example of this happens when I'm at work - I work at an Italian restaurant, and my job is to make all the drinks for dine-in, and all desserts for both dine-in and takeaway. There is a printer in front of me that prints people's drink orders when the waitresses have put them through, and that alone isn't a problem; it works perfectly.

Unfortunately the printer prints all orders that contain a drink or a dessert, whether it's relevant to me or not - which means that whenever the printer prints a takeaway order for a bottle of Coke for example, I just take the docket, stick it straight onto the spike, and go back to whatever I was doing. Another problem is that the bar printer also prints dockets containing any 'dummy' items used for price adjustment for UberEats orders, which come to me as nonsense orders (There might be an order of "U" for $0.90, for example).

I don't know who designed the system that decides which items go to which printer but whenever I spike an order that doesn't need to have come to me, I die a little inside, both at the waste of paper and at the inefficiency of whatever database system decides which items get printed where, and I always want to fix it. To do that, I would have to do the following:

  1. Investigate how the system determines what goes where - it's important to note that my printer is the only printer that needs changing, as the pizza and kitchen printers need to receive every order for their category regardless.
  2. Maybe create some algorithm that uses the combination of the type of item (food, drink, pizza, dessert, etc) and also the eating mode (dine-in, takeaway, UberEats), and prints accordingly - the bar printer should receive only dine-in drink orders, and dessert orders regardless of eating mode.
  3. Perhaps add an extra category called "dummy order" for the dummy price adjustment items that doesn't get printed anywhere.
  4. Change and test the system by putting through dummy test orders for:
    • Every item individually, in both dine-in and takeaway/UE forms
    • Random combinations of items, again in both eat in and eat out modes
    • Dummy price adjusters in some orders, distributed randomly
    • Orders that have had changes made - something added, excluded, or substituted from an order, for all food types (pizza, kitchen food, drinks)
  5. Fix any errors if something gets printed to the wrong place or not at all.

As I haven't looked at the language the system currently uses, I don't actually know how exactly to accomplish this task, nor will I be able to find out any time soon as the restaurant is mostly closed due to the current situation in Victoria. Regardless, if this were done it would certainly make my life easier as the printer wouldn't print things I didn't have to worry about, and presumably other restaurants under the same franchise use a similar or identical system, and their systems could be improved too. Depending on how widespread the system is, it could even be used to improve efficiency in other restaurants as well.
